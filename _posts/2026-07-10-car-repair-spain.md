*An objective decision model that balances market potential against operating costs to identify the most promising Spanish provinces.*

Choosing where to open a business is one of the most expensive decisions an entrepreneur makes. Large cities provide access to more customers, but they also come with higher rents, higher living costs, and stronger competition. Smaller cities reduce operating expenses, yet often lack sufficient demand to sustain long-term growth.

Rather than relying on intuition or isolated metrics, this project develops a mathematical model that evaluates multiple socioeconomic factors simultaneously. Vehicle fleet size, population density, income, rent, unemployment, and cost of living are combined into a single index that measures the trade-off between market opportunity and operating costs.

Applied to the 50 continental provinces of Spain, the model identified not only the largest markets, but also regions offering a more attractive balance between profitability, stability, and business risk. The analysis provides an objective starting point for entrepreneurs before conducting local market research.
