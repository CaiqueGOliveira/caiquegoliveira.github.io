*A mathematical model that identifies the price maximizing long-term profitability rather than intuition or guesswork.*

Pricing is one of the most difficult decisions any business faces. Setting prices too high may reduce demand, while prices that are too low can increase sales but leave significant profit on the table. Small pricing mistakes, repeated over thousands of transactions, can have a substantial financial impact.

This project develops a mathematical model that estimates the relationship between price and customer demand using historical sales data. Rather than maximizing revenue alone, the model also incorporates operating costs to identify the price that maximizes actual profit.

Applied to a real coffee shop pricing scenario, the model identified an optimal selling price together with a practical pricing interval that remains close to maximum profitability while providing flexibility for business decisions.
